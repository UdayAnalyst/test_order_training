PURCHASE ORDER ENTRY AND CONFIRMATION

Contract Review

Upon receiving a purchase order from a customer, Strategic Accounts will date stamp the order and review for accuracy. The main things you’ll make sure are correct are the company (Okay Industries US – Berlin / New Britain), part number, revision, piece price, Net Terms, Ship To address, Bill To address, IncoTerms (FOB Shipping Point / Collect / ExWorks). You’ll verify that these have been checked by placing a checkmark on the purchase order at each item. Once verified, you will add your signature at the bottom confirming Contract Review is complete.

  • Note any discrepancies under your signature , so that you can go back to the customer for modifications.

Entering Order in Plex

Once Contract Review is complete, the purchase order will be entered into Plex UX.

After signing into Plex, go to your Sales – UX Role-Based Menu, and click on Customer Purchase Orders.





















At the Customer Purchase Order screen, click on Add.







Once you have successfully logged in to Plex, you will see your Launch Page. Note that your page may look different than others, depending on your role. It will also change once you start to customize.



































This screen is where you will enter the general PO details, taken directly from the customer purchase order. Once everything is filled out, you will click on ‘Apply’.







Fields:

Customer – Customer Code / Company that sent the PO.

Customer PO No – This is the purchase order number.

Order Status – Leave as ‘Open’.

Order Type – This will be ‘New Order’ until Production confirms dates / quantities.

Order Date – This is the date we received the PO from the customer.

Expiration Date – This can be left blank.

Terms – This should automatically populate once the Customer field is filled out.

F.O.B. – Leave as ‘Okay Ind’.

Freight Terms – Select ‘Collect’ from the drop down list.

Note – This is where we summarize the part number and quantity being ordered (ex: 412 ASY – 45,200 pieces), and any other important notes pertaining to the order.

Contact – This can be left blank.

Contact Note – This can be left blank.

Certifications – This can be left blank.

Shipping Instructions – This can be left blank.

Shipper Note – This can be left blank.

Next, you’ll click on ‘Add PO Line’.








Here, you’ll fill in ‘Part No’, check the ‘New Shipper Per Schedule’ box, choose the ‘Approved Ship To’ address, fill in the ‘Ship From’ field (Berlin or New Britain), and click ‘Apply’. Note that more than one ‘Approved Ship To’ address can be chosen.























Sales Department Quick Links



Once that is complete, you’ll click on ‘Add price’.



Here, you will fill out the ‘Price/Unit’ field with the piece price quoted, and in the ‘Note’ field, you will enter the quote number referenced (ex: Quote 215139 R39). The ‘Effective Date’ should be the date of the Purchase Order, not the date entered. Then, click ‘Ok’.























Once that is complete, you’ll click on ‘Add Release’.











On the Release Detail screen, you are responsible for filling out the following sections:

  • S

    In the ‘Shipment Details’ section, the ‘Ship To’ and ‘Ship From’ fields should be auto populated with what you chose for the ‘Approved Ship To’ address. If there was more than one ‘Ship To’ option, make sure the correct one is chosen. The ‘Release No’ field is for any notes you want to add specific to that line item (ex: if the parts are shipping to someone’s attention, if the line is for samples/lot charges, etc.)

    hipment Details, Release Details, Notes (if applicable)













In the ‘Release Details’ section, you will need to fill out ‘Quantity’ (how many parts we’ll be shipping against this line), ‘Due Date’ (when the customer is requesting to have parts delivered), and ‘Release Type’ (since this is a new order that hasn’t been confirmed by Production yet, the Release Type will be ‘New Order’. This will be modified later).









In the ‘Notes’ section, you can add any notes that pertain to this specific release line. ‘Note’ will only be shown internally, ‘Printed Note’ can be printed on the customer paperwork.























If there is more than one release, you will hit ‘Apply’, and then ‘Add Release’ at the top of the screen. If this is the only line item, you’ll hit ‘Ok’.
Notify Planning you entered the order.









To verify that the order was entered correctly, go to your Launch Page, and in the top right corner, type Order Summary in the Search Bar.





















In the ‘Customer PO’ field, type in the purchase order number of the PO you entered (ex: 20164103). Delete what is in the ‘Due Date’ field and hit ‘Search’.



















This will give you a snapshot of the releases entered, with pricing.











When Planning Confirms the Order

  • Update the PO Order Type:

  • Enter the “Date Confirmed”

  • Update Release dates if necessary. Change each Release Type to “FIRM”

  • Confirm to the customer

  • Attach the confirmation to the PO in Plex

FINAL KNOWLEDGE CHECK

Test your purchase-order entry knowledge

Select one answer for each question, then submit your answers.

1. What should the Order Type be before Production confirms the order?
Correct answer: New Order. It remains New Order until Production confirms dates and quantities.
2. Which date should be entered as the price Effective Date?
Correct answer: the Purchase Order date, not the date the information is entered.
3. After Planning confirms the order, what should each Release Type be changed to?
Correct answer: FIRM. Update release dates if needed, then change every Release Type to FIRM.
